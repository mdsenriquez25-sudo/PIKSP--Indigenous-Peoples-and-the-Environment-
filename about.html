Realities in the Lands Where Indigenous Peoples Lived.

Land rights in law, but not in reality

•Some countries recognize indigenous land rights, but many are not fully implemented.

•Governments lease lands for mining or logging without proper consent.

New technologies force resettlement

•Use of chemical fertilizers, pesticides, and cash-crop farming damages the environment.

•Indigenous communities are forced to leave their lands.

The cost of unsustainable development

•Large dams and mining displace indigenous families.

•Many suffer abuse, imprisonment, or even death when defending their rights.

Implementation gap: Much talk, little action

•Indigenous rights are recognized internationally.

•But many countries fail to apply these rights locally.

Climate change threatens their existence

•Climate change directly affects indigenous communities.

•It threatens their survival, livelihoods, and human rights.

Severe impact on women

•Indigenous women suffer more from displacement.

•They travel farther for water and fuel.

•Many lose income and become economically dependent.
