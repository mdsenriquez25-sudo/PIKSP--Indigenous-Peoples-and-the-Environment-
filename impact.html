Causes of Biodiversity Loss in Indigenous Lands in the Philippines

• Logging. Long-term logging has damaged forests by reducing species diversity and disrupting natural energy, water, and nutrient cycles.

• Small-scale upland agriculture. Slash-and-burn farming was once sustainable with crop rotation, but it became unsustainable as increasing numbers of landless migrants overused the land.

• Over-hunting. Unregulated hunting for food, training, or recreation harms biodiversity and disrupts ecosystem balance by preventing wildlife from fulfilling their natural roles.

• Illegal fishing. Destructive fishing methods and overfishing cause biodiversity loss in aquatic ecosystems by damaging habitats, killing marine life, and reducing the productivity of rivers and fishing grounds.

• Forest fire. During extreme heat temperature, our forest can reach its kindling temperature and a fire starts.

• Improper grazing. Ruminants placed in a pasture land where they just freely roam around in open farmland can cause damage or destruction to other species of the flora and fauna.

• Construction and conversion of forest areas into residential lots. The construction and conversion of forest areas into residential lots has threatened indigenous species with the loss of their natural habitat and breeding environment.
